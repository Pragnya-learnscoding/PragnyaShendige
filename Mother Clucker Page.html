Mother Clucker

Card Game Design | Concept Design | Storytelling

Duration: 3 Weeks | 2024
Team of 2

Can’t you tell? It’s a mother clucking card game! My best friend and I gave 'Exploding Kittens' a desi makeover with partying Murgis. It’s a reverse deck-building compact box of endless entertainment you can play in a cozy game night with friends.

Game Overview

We focused on keeping the core gameplay of Exploding Kittens familiar and fast-paced, applying a fun desi theme (Murgis vs. Maasi!) without needing extensive playtesting changes. However, we did introduce an interesting twist with our deck-building mechanic.

Play Testing Insights

Winning Strategy (or maybe not)

Our initial idea was a simpler approach inspired by Go Fish. When a player used a "Heist Card" to request a Key Card, the targeted player had to hand it over if they had one. This quickly backfired in playtesting. The game became predictable and ended too quickly, lacking the fun tension we were aiming for.

Introducing the Art of Bluff

This is where our winning mechanic emerged! We turned to the classic game of bluff for this one. We introduced the element of bluffing. Now, when a player uses a Heist Card, the targeted player has a choice:

  • Truthful:They can reveal if they have a Key Card and hand it over, fostering trust within the murghi flock.
  • Deceptive: They can strategically lie and claim they don’t have a Key Card, even if they do. This deception could be fueled by selfishness (aiming for a solo win) or distrust towards other players.

Consequences of a Bluff

If a player chooses to bluff and deny having a Key Card, the player who requested it (using the Heist Card) has to draw two cards from the deck. This "cat and mouse" dynamic within the murghi team injects a delightful layer of suspicion and potential betrayal, making Mother Clucker a truly clucking good time!

We also realized that the mausi player would always know not to give away their key cards since they will be the lone mausi in the game, but we felt that particular advantage was fair as it was 1 mausi against 4 murgis throughout the game.

Game Concept

Epic Story

The game concept revolved around creating a hilarious, easy-to-pick-up card game with a cultural twist. Our target was to design a game that brought people together for a laugh while still providing strategic gameplay.

Wildest Cards

A bunch of loony characters populate the game, each with their own quirky agendas but all bound by one common objective: taking down the dreaded Mother Clucker!
